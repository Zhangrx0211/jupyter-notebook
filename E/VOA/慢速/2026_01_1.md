A small experiment shows that mindfulness training exercises may help people who drink large amounts of alcohol.

Mindfulness is a method of quietly, but fully, experiencing what is happening in the present, both in and around you.

The mindfulness study involved 68 heavy drinkers in the United Kingdom.

The subjects were not alcoholics, but they did have more alcohol to drink than most people.

Some of the subjects received training in relaxation methods.

The other subjects took part in an 11-minute training program in mindfulness exercises.

The program was designed to help them recognize their desire for a drink without acting on it.

Over the next week, the group that received mindfulness training drank much less than they had during the week before the study started.

But the subjects in the relaxation group did not drink much less.

Damla Irez is with University College London.

She says the study did not involve 'treating' people who needed help cutting down their alcohol use.

"But it did suggest that people who drink too much, but don't have an alcohol use disorder, might be able to reduce their consumption, at least in the short term, by practicing mindfulness," Irez said by email.

The Reuters news agency reported her comments.

A report on the study was published in The International Journal of Neuropsychopharmacology.

During the mindfulness training, people were told to pay attention to their desire to drink instead of ignoring this feeling.

They were told that, by noticing bodily sensations, they could accept them as temporary events without needing to act on them.

The other group – those in relaxation training – was told that softening the muscles, calming and unwinding the mind and releasing tension in the body could reduce the desire to drink.
而另一组接受放松训练的参与者则被告知，通过放松肌肉、平静心灵以及缓解身体紧张，可以减少对饮酒的渴望。
After receiving one of these trainings, all 68 people were asked to use the techniques they learned for one week.
在接受了这些培训之后，所有68名参与者都被要求在接下来的一周内运用他们所学到的技巧。
Right after training, both groups reported a reduced desire for a drink although the reduction was greater in the relaxation group.
培训结束后，两组参与者都表示自己对饮酒的欲望有所减弱，不过放松组的这种变化更为明显。
However, people in the mindfulness group drank 9.3 fewer units of alcohol in the week after the training than they did in the week before the study began.
然而，在正念训练组中，参与者在培训后的一周内饮酒量比研究开始前的一周减少了9.3单位的酒精。
This is about equal to three pints of beer – or about 13,000 milliliters.
这大约相当于三品脱啤酒的量，也就是约13000毫升。
In the relaxation group, people consumed just 3 fewer units of alcohol.
而在放松组中，参与者仅减少了3单位的酒精摄入量。
This difference was too small to rule out the possibility that the cause was chance.
这种差异太小，无法排除这种变化纯属偶然的可能性。
One limitation of the study was the small number of subjects.
这项研究的一个局限性在于参与者的数量较少。
Another limitation was that the researchers depended on the subjects to remember and report how much alcohol they drank.
另一个局限性在于，研究人员需要依赖参与者自己来回忆并报告他们的饮酒情况。
Researchers also depended on them to be honest about whether they had been diagnosed with alcohol use disorder.
同时也要依赖参与者自己如实说明他们是否被诊断出患有酒精使用障碍。
In addition, the study was too brief to show how much training people might require to see long-term changes to their drinking behavior.
此外，这项研究的持续时间太短，无法说明人们需要接受多少次这样的培训才能看到其饮酒行为的长期改变。
William Marchand is a psychiatry professor at the University of Utah in Salt Lake City.
威廉·马尚是位于盐湖城的犹他大学的精神病学教授。
He wasn't involved in the British study.
他并未参与这项英国的研究。
But he said it was new and surprising that such a short training period would have an effect on behavior.
但他表示，如此短时间的培训就能对行为产生影响，这确实令人感到新奇和惊讶。
In an email to Reuters, Marchard said he would have thought much more training would be needed to change behavior.
在给路透社的电子邮件中，马尚表示，他原本认为需要更长时间的培训才能改变人们的饮酒行为。
"What we don't know," he said, "is whether there will be a lasting impact on drinking behavior."
“我们目前还不知道，”他说，“这种改变是否会对饮酒行为产生持久的影响。”
Still, the first step in targeting addictive behaviors is to notice them, said Stefan Hofmann, a psychology researcher at Boston University in Massachusetts.
不过，马萨诸塞州波士顿大学的心理学研究员斯特凡·霍夫曼指出，要针对成瘾行为，第一步就是首先要意识到这些行为的存在。
Hofmann told Reuters in an email that humans usually develop habits and link behaviors to situational signals.
霍夫曼在给路透社的电子邮件中写道，人类通常会形成某些习惯，并将某些行为与特定的情境信号联系起来。
For example, for drinkers, this might mean that being around certain people or in places, such as a bar, will make drinking more probable, he said.
例如，对于饮酒者来说，这可能意味着在某些人身边或处于某些特定环境中（比如酒吧），他们更有可能饮酒。
Ordering another drink can become automatic in these situations, but becoming watchful of signs that lead to more drinking can help change how people react to these signs.
在这些情况下，再次点酒的行为可能会变得自动化，但通过警惕那些可能导致更多饮酒的信号，人们就可以改变自己对这些信号的反应方式。
"This is why mindfulness can be a very powerful strategy," Hofmann said.
“这就是为什么正念训练可以成为一种非常有效的策略，”霍夫曼说。
He said it stops automatic behavior by making people more thoughtful.
他认为，正念训练通过让人们更加深思熟虑，从而阻止了那些自动化的行为反应。
